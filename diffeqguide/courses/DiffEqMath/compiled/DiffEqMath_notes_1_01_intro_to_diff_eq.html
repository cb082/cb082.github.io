Concepts

An ordinary differential equation (ODE) is an equation consisting of an independent variable, say \(x\), a dependent variable, say \(y\), and derivatives of the dependent variable with respect to the independent variable. Very formally it can be written to look like \[f(x, y, y', \dots, y^{(n)}) = 0\] for some positive integer \(n\).
The following are examples of ODEs:
  • \(y' = y\)
  • \(x^2y'' - xy' + y = \cos(x)\)
An autonomous ODE is an ODE that has no explicit dependence on the dependent variable.
