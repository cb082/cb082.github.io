Convolution

Given piecewise continuous functions \(f\) and \(g\) on \([0, \infty)\), the convolution of \(f\) and \(g\) is given by \[(f\ast g)(t) = \int_{0}^{t} f(\tau)g(t - \tau)\,d\tau.\]
Given piecewise continuous functions \(f\), \(g\), and \(h\) on \([0, \infty)\), we have that
  • \(f \ast g = g \ast f\)
  • \((f + g)\ast h = f \ast h + g \ast h\)
  • \(\mathcal{L}\{f \ast g\} = \mathcal{L}\{f\} \cdot \mathcal{L}\{g\}\)
